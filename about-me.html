ABOUT ME

I’ve heard that imagination combined with knowledge is one of the most powerful tools in a person’s arsenal. Some even call it magic. So, my twenty-one-year-old self, Nijaz Andelić, wants to become the best and most creative wizard possible.

Watch out Dumbledore

SSST & LEGO

I am a Computer Science student at the Sarajevo School of Science and Technology. I first encountered Lego bricks at the age of five, which means I have around 15 years of experience in problem-solving. Joking aside, I've shown an interest in engineering since childhood through these Lego bricks and the influence of an older friend who is now a senior software engineer.

  • My cousins often teased me by turning off my joystick while they were gaming— a funny experience.

  • During elementary school, I delved deeper into computers through games (P.S. I played all the good games at my software engineering friend’s house because I had a “junk” computer).

  • However, that junk computer had a huge impact on my career path. Playing Minecraft Hunger Games, experiencing game lag during fights was not pleasant and made me search “How to speed up Minecraft” for the first time. This led to new problems like needing more RAM, GPU… But, what is RAM? And so I became a googler (What is RAM? How to get more RAM without buying parts? FPS? GPU?...). That’s where my problem-solving and googling skills began. Long story short, one of my hobbies today is assembling computers and computer configurations.

  • I enrolled in high school for electrical engineering because of Computer Science, and from extremely traumatic experiences, my love for programming was born.

  • I remember well that in the second grade of high school, while typing double nested loops, I said these loops would never be useful for programming because it’s Pascal… Ah, the good old days… And so, officially, in the summer of 2019, I wrote my first independent line of code—believe it or not—in Python. A language I still don’t like today (I’m more of a Java guy, or maybe because Python it’s a good rival to my JavaScript).

  • I quickly moved away from Python and started a career in Web Development. Despite many breaks, mistakes, failures, and moments of doubt, I managed to train my brain to work when it’s hardest… Of course, this wouldn’t be possible if I didn’t love programming.

  • So, I firmly say, programming is Me, and I am a programmer. I love engineering and optimization, so I even pretend to be Dijkstra in my head, finding the shortest possible routes (calculating constraints by traffic lights) to get somewhere the fastest—I consider it algorithm practice.

  • As a child, I said I wanted to be a “Doctor for computers”, and to this day, I aim for that target (A wizard with a gun—interesting).

  • To keep it short, the magic wand I am currently improving the most is React. I love creating magic in React, but I also work with other frontend technologies (React, React Native, JavaScript, TypeScript, PHP, HTML, CSS…).

  • One of my best React projects is the Dono app, which you can check out. Other projects are on my GitHub and this page, and I advise you to take a look at them.
